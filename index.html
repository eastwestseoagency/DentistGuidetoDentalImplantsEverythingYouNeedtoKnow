Understanding Dental Implants

Dental implants are permanent tooth replacement solutions designed to replicate the structure of natural teeth. Unlike dentures or bridges, dental implants replace both the tooth root and the visible tooth, providing superior strength and stability.

A complete dental implant consists of:

A titanium implant post placed in the jawbone

An abutment that connects the implant to the crown

A custom crown for implant tooth restoration

Dental implants integrate directly with the jawbone, a process known as osseointegration, which allows them to function just like natural teeth.

The Importance of a Dental Implants Periodontist

A dental implants periodontist is a specialist who focuses on gum health and the supporting bone structures around teeth and implants. Since successful dental implant placement depends heavily on healthy gums and strong bone, a periodontist may be involved in cases that require advanced surgical expertise.

A dental implants periodontist commonly assists with:

Bone grafting procedures

Treatment of gum disease

Complex tooth implant surgery

Managing soft tissue around implants
